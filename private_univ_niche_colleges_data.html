5 College Junior 14 days ago

Overall Experience: Hendrix College is a primarily residential school, and I am an untraditional student. I will be starting my junior year in a couple of months, and I have a two year old son. The balance has made school harder, but everyone there, from staff to students wants me to succeed. It really is a family there. Senior year of high school when my principal knew I was considering going, invited me to his office to convince me, and will still help me if I need anything. Some of the guys and ladies in the cafeteria have worked there for over thirty years, they learn your names, ask how you're doing, become friends with you, and consider you family. They recognize people that come back to visit 20+ years later, and one of them has even given me holiday gifts for my son. The Deans and President of the college are almost ALWAYS on campus, and come up and talk to students regularly. It's not unusual to see one of the deans with his kids playing a video game in the student area or the president and his wife, who is also a professor, walking their dog on campus when not at work. It's the same way with professors, and not unusual to be doing your workout with one of them. The school encourages you to explore new things and classes with it's collegiate requirements, and you meet a lot of people with different backgrounds. The school has several international students, and half of my class alone is from out of state. There's no shortage of things to do on campus, and between going to club sponsored events and department talks, you could almost get by without a meal plan or buying food. If you're having trouble in classes, there are plenty of tutors and professors will work one on one with you, and it's typically not hard to get an appointment. Since the class size is kept smaller you can't be lost in the system, and I know that despite whatever might be going on, I always have someone in my corner to help me succeed.

Overall ExperienceWhat's this?

View student contributors

Hendrix College may not be for everybody, but for many students, it’s a wonderful choice. It’s a small school, which comes with both pros and cons. The downside is that there are less classes and majors to choose from, and that if you make any enemies, you probably won’t be able to avoid them until one of you graduates. The upside is that Hendrix is a tight-knit community where you’ll recognize many familiar faces on your way to and from classes, bonding over the popular annual events scattered across every students’ time at Hendrix. This community feel extends outside of the campus—alumni are friendly toward other Hendrix graduates, and even non-alumni in the Arkansas area generally have good impressions of Hendrix.

There’s always something to do at Hendrix, be it shopping at the ever-popular Walmart, taking advantage of the various off-campus dining options, seeing a movie, going to a club, or listening to a visiting lecturer. The faculty and staff are all friendly, caring, and truly devoted to helping you personally succeed at Hendrix. The food is great, with lots of options for even the pickiest eaters. The dorms can be crowded, but they’re also community-builders. Conway is a great small town, and Little Rock’s presence nearby doesn’t hurt, either, though Hendrix’s Central Arkansas location means some crazy weather and being in a dry county can be off-putting to some partiers. Public transportation may be nonexistent, but Hendrix makes it easy to have a car on campus, and many things are within walking distance anyway. When some of the biggest complaints about the school include the lack of proper drainage and the intermittent train whistles, you know you’ve got it made!
